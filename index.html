Start/End

mutant has classes for starting and ending on a particular row or column. col-start-#, col-end-#, row-start-# and row-end-# will dictate this, no -span- needed.

Note: CSS Grid's tracks start before the first column in your grid and end after the last. This 8-column grid starts on track 1 and ends on track 9. Open up your developer tools (Firefox Developer Edition has very comprehensive grid tools) and take a look. The numbers are the order of the items, then the starting - ending columns, then rows.

1. 3 - 5
2. 3 - 5
3. end 6, 9
4. 4 - 7, 2 - 5
5. start 1, 4 - 6
6. 7 - 9, 1 - 3
7. 3 - 5, 2 - 4
8. 2 - 4, 3 - 5

Oops. Forgot to mention, you can layer items this way. Well, now you know.

Auto-fit/Auto-fill

You can also set your columns and rows to auto-fit or auto-fill:

  • Auto-fit fits the columns into the space, expanding them to take up any available space in the grid.
  • Auto-fill fills the row with as many columns as it can fit, creating implicit columns when a new column can fit.
